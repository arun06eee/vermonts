Black Pepper

Black pepper is the dried mature berry of piper nigrum, a climbing, perennial shrub mostly found in hot, moist region of southern India. Under cultivation pepper vines are trailed over support as columns, 5-6mtr tall, 1-2 meter diameter. The Climbing woody stems have swollen nods with clinging roots at each node, which helps in anchoring the vine to the support trees. It has straight upward growing main stem and have lateral shoots from the axils of the Leaves having shorter inter nodes without adventitious roots. In such branches the terminal buds get modified into an inflorescence (spike) an auxiliary buds continue further growth.

The root system confined to 75-100 cm radius and depth. The inflorescence is a pendent spike, 3-15cm long with 50-15- flowers. Flowers are minute, white pale yellow, arranged spirally on fleshy pedantries. The specie is naturally self-pollinated and pollen dispersal is aided by the presence of water droplets. Fruit is a single seeded drupe often called berry. It is spherical in shape, green in color, changing to red on ripping. Its botanical Name is Piper Nigrum L and belongs to family of Piperaceae.
