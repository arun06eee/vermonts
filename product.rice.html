Rice

Rice is the seed of the grass species Oryza Sativa (Asian rice) or Oryza glaberrima (Africian rice). As a cereal grain, it is the most widely consumed staple food for a large part of the world’s human population.

Basmati Rice

“Basmati” is long grain aromatic rice grown for many centuries in the specific geographical area, at the Himalayan foot hills of India sub-continent, blessed with characteristics extra-long slender grains that elongate at least twice of their original size with a characteristics soft and fluffy texture upon cooking, delicious taste, superior aroma and distinct flavor, Basmati rice is unique among other aromatic long grain rice varieties.

Non- Basmati Rice

Rice other than basmati rice is called non-basmati rice. In the world it has been reported that there are 1000 varieties of rice, the maximum number being in India. In fact, basmati rice equals to only 1% production of the total rice grown in India. Non-basmati rice comes in all kinds of different shapes and sizes. Some are long and slender, some are short and thick, some are like beads and some may be round. None have the same characteristics as basmati rice. Some names of non- basmati rice are shaped like basmati rice and PR,104 IR8, IR64 and Masuri.
